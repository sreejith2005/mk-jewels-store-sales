## INTRODUCTION: GOLD JEWELLERY IS A TRANSFORMATION, NOT ASSEMBLY 

Gold jewellery does not begin as a product. 

It begins as a pure gold bar. 

To transform this bar into wearable, durable, certified jewellery, gold must pass through multiple irreversible processes. 

At every stage, a small quantity of gold is permanently lost. 

This unavoidable gold loss is called wastage — and wastage is the core reason making charges increase when gold prices rise. 

--- 

PROCESS 1: MELTING & ALLOYING LOSS 

What Happens 

Pure gold is melted at 1200°C+ 

Alloys (silver, copper, zinc, etc.) are mixed to create 18K / 22K / 14K gold 

At extreme temperatures: 

Gold evaporates as vapour 

Microscopic particles burn off 

Nature of Loss 

Uncontrollable 

Irrecoverable 

Happens before jewellery even takes shape 

Why It Matters 

As gold value rises, even microscopic loss becomes financially significant. 

--- 

## PROCESS 2: CASTING LOSS 

What Happens 

Molten gold is poured into wax or rubber moulds 

Gold flows through channels and cavities 

Nature of Loss 

Gold remains inside: 

Sprues 

Casting trees 

Mould residue 

Only partial recovery is possible 

Why It Matters 

Precision casting ensures strength and finish—but precision comes with loss. 

--- 

PROCESS 3: CUTTING & FILING LOSS 

What Happens 

Excess gold is trimmed 

Edges are refined 

Shapes are perfected manually 

Nature of Loss 

Gold is removed as fine powder 

Powder is: 

Too thin 

Mixed with dust 

Impossible to fully collect or refine 

Why It Matters 

This step ensures comfort, symmetry, and elegance. 

--- 

PROCESS 4: STONE SETTING LOSS 

What Happens 

Grooves and prongs are cut into gold 

Gold is displaced to secure stones safely 

Nature of Loss 

Gold is removed permanently 

Safety and durability demand precision 

Why It Matters 

Secure stone setting protects the customer from future loss or damage. 

--- 

## PROCESS 5: POLISHING LOSS 

What Happens 

Jewellery is polished for shine and smoothness 

Surface layers are refined 

Nature of Loss 

Micron-level gold removal 

Gold dust disperses into polishing compounds 

Why It Matters 

This is what gives jewellery its final luxury finish.

PROCESS 6: DESIGN, CAD & QUALITY CONTROL (NON-GOLD COST) 

What Happens 

Concept creation

Prototype approvals 

Final quality inspection

This is where jewellery becomes art, not commodity.
