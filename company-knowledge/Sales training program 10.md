MK JEWELS – MASTER SALES SOP 

“Sell Emotion. Justify with Logic. Close with Trust.” 

--- 

SECTION 1: SALES MINDSET (FOUNDATION) 

Rule 1: People Buy Emotion, Then Justify with Logic 

## What this means: 

Customers fall in love first. Logic is used only to convince family members later. 

Salesperson Script: 

> “Ma’am, this piece is not just jewellery — it’s how you’ll feel when you wear it. Confident. Complete. Special.” 

Then add logic: 

> “And of course, it’s also 18kt certified, perfectly weighted, and easy to maintain.” 

--- 

Rule 2: You’re Not Selling Jewellery. You’re Selling Transformation. 

Transformation Examples: 

From ordinary → elegant 

From unsure → confident 

From confused → admired 

Script: 

> “This isn’t about buying a necklace. It’s about how people will look at you when you walk in wearing this.” 

--- 

SECTION 2: OPENING THE CONVERSATION (FIRST 30 SECONDS) 

Rule 3: Ask, Don’t Pitch 

Never start with: 

❌ “What are you looking for?” 

Start with: 

> “Who are you buying this for?” 

“Is this for a special occasion or everyday elegance?” 

“What kind of look do you usually feel most confident in?” 

This creates emotional permission. 

--- 

Rule 4: Silence is a Sales Tool 

After quoting price — stay silent. 

Script: 

> “This piece is 3,85,000.”₹ (Stop talking. Let silence work.) 

Silence = confidence. 

--- 

## SECTION 3: VALUE CREATION (PSYCHOLOGY-DRIVEN) 

Rule 5: Sell Relief, Not Features 

People don’t want gold. 

They want relief from doubt, fear, and regret. 

Script: 

> “This design works with everything — weddings, functions, even small get-togethers. You’ll never feel ‘overdressed’ or ‘underdressed’.” 

--- 

Rule 6: Specificity Creates Trust 

Bad: 

> “Very good quality, madam.” 

Good: 

> “This is 18kt gold, handcrafted, with reinforced prongs so the stones don’t loosen even after years.” 

--- 

Rule 7: Use Social Proof Correctly 

Never say: “Many people buy this.” 

Say instead: 

> “Clients who prefer subtle elegance usually choose this design.” “A bride last week selected this for her engagement.” 

--- 

## SECTION 4: HANDLING PRICE OBJECTIONS 

Rule 8: Price Is Never the Real Objection 

Script: 

> “I understand. Before we talk about price, may I ask—what matters most to you in this piece?” 

Then connect price to value: 

> “This costs more because it saves you future regret.” 

--- 

Rule 9: Show Cost of Not Buying 

> “Many clients come back saying they wish they had taken this when it was available.” 

Fear of loss > desire to gain. 

--- 

SECTION 5: BUILDING TRUST & AUTHORITY 

Rule 10: Proof Over Promise 

Use: 

Real customer photos 

Certificates 

Store legacy 

Years of experience 

## Script: 

> “We’ve been trusted by families for generations. That trust is what we protect.” 

--- 

Rule 11: Be a Consultant, Not a Seller 

> “If this were for my own family, I’d suggest this piece.” 

This instantly lowers resistance. 

--- 

SECTION 6: CLOSING THE SALE (THE RIGHT WAY) 

Rule 12: Offer a Soft Close 

> “Would you like to try this once?” 

“Shall I show you how it looks with your outfit?” 

Never ask “Do you want to buy?” 

--- 

Rule 13: Create Safety 

> “You can take your time. There’s no rush at all.” 

Paradoxically increases conversion. 

--- 

Rule 14: Anchor the Decision 

- “This is one of those pieces you’ll never regret owning.” 

--- 

## SECTION 7: POST-SALE TRUST BUILDING 

Rule 15: Reinforce the Decision 

After purchase: 

- “You made a beautiful choice. This will always remain special.” 

Rule 16: Create Relationship, Not Transaction 

- “Anytime you need styling advice, please message me directly.” 

--- 

SECTION 8: GOLDEN RULES FOR MK JEWELS SALES TEAM 

1. Don’t sell products. Sell emotions. 

2. Don’t talk too much. Ask better questions. 

3. Don’t discount value. Increase meaning. 

4. Don’t rush. Guide. 

5. Don’t argue. Align. 

6. Don’t push. Invite. 

7. Don’t explain features. Translate feelings. 

8. Don’t chase customers. Attract them. 

--- 

FINAL MANTRA FOR SALES TEAM 

> “People forget prices. They never forget how you made them feel.”
