Below is a structured, floor-ready sales script your MK Jewels sales team can use when a customer says “Aapki making charges zyada hain.” The flow is intentional: Agree → Clarify comparison → Reframe value → Justify making → Emotional payoff → Soft close with expert offer (not discount positioning). 

--- 

## 1. Agree First (Disarm, Don’t Defend) 

Salesperson (calm, respectful tone, slight nod): 

“Ji ma’am, main samajh sakta hoon. Aapko iss product ki making thodi zyada lag rahi hai.” 

(Pause. Let the customer feel heard.) 

--- 

2. Ask the Right Question (Shift from Price to Comparison) 

## Salesperson: 

“Main sirf ek cheez samajhna chahta hoon ma’am, taaki main aapko sahi tarah guide kar sakoon — 

Aap iski making kis se compare kar rahi hain? 

- Koi pehle kharida hua piece? 

- Kisi aur jeweller ka design? 

- Ya online jo aapne dekha ho?” 

(This question changes the conversation from objection to discussion.) 

--- 

## 3. Acknowledge Comparison (No Judgement) 

Salesperson: 

“Bilkul, comparison natural hai ma’am. Jewellery mein price ka farq aksar design, craftsmanship aur process ki wajah se hota hai — sirf weight ki wajah se nahi.” 

--- 

4. Explain the Value (Craft, Time, Art) 

(Pick up the piece gently, show details.) 

Salesperson: 

“Ma’am, yeh sirf jewellery nahi hai — yeh handcrafted piece of art hai. Is ek piece ko banane mein approx 400–500 working hours lagte hain.” 

“Ismein machine ka role kam aur karigar ke haath ka kaam zyada hota hai — detailing, balance, finishing, aur comfort sab manually check hota hai.” 

--- 

5. Explain the Processes (Where Making Actually Goes) 

Salesperson (structured, confident): 

“Is piece ne sirf ek nahi, multiple processes cross kiye hain: 

- Design finalisation 

- Casting / hand fabrication 

- Stone setting / finishing 

- Polishing & symmetry check 

- Hallmarking 

- Quality certification 

- Final fitting & comfort test 

Har stage pe gold loss hota hai, skill lagti hai, aur accountability hoti hai.” 

(Optional line) 

“Isliye ma’am, making sirf ‘charge’ nahi hoti — yeh process aur expertise ki value hoti hai.” 

--- 

6. Personalise the Value (Why THIS Piece for HER) 

Salesperson (soft, appreciative tone): 

“Ma’am, yeh design aapke neck / hand structure pe bahut achha lagega. 

Iski finishing aisi hai ki jab aap pehenti hain, toh log sirf gold nahi — design aur class notice karte hain.” 

“Is type ka piece aap har jagah repeat nahi dekhenge. Isliye jab aap pehenengi, compliments guaranteed honge.” 

--- 

7. Reframe Cost into Longevity & Pride 

Salesperson: 

“Making ek baar lagti hai ma’am, par yeh feeling, comfort aur look saalon tak rehta hai.” 

“Yeh wo jewellery hai jo aaj bhi strong lagegi, aur 10 saal baad bhi outdated nahi lagegi.” 

--- 

8. Close with an OFFER (Not Discount Language) 

(Very important: don’t say “discount”. Say “expert privilege” or “special consideration”.) 

Salesperson (warm smile): 

“Ma’am, agar aap is piece ke saath proceed karti hain, toh MK Jewels ke taraf se, hum aapko ek special expert consideration denge.” 

“Is particular piece pe, making charges mein 10% ka adjustment hum aapke liye kar denge — 

kyunki hum chahte hain ki aap value ke saath comfortable decision lein.” 

(Pause, then close softly) 

“Shall I keep this piece aside for you and proceed?” 

--- 

9. Body Language & Delivery Notes (For Training) 

Voice: Calm, confident, never defensive 

Hands: Open gestures, hold jewellery carefully 

Eye contact: While explaining value, not price 

Smile: Especially while offering the expert consideration 

Never rush the offer — let value land first
