## **Understanding Tunch & Wastage in the Gold Jewellery Business** 

_A clear explanation for customers & staff_ 

## **1. What is Tunch (Touch / Purity)** 

**Tunch** refers to the **purity of gold** present in a jewellery item. 

It is expressed as: 

- **Percentage** (e.g. 91.6%, 75%) 

- **Karat** (e.g. 22K, 18K) 

## **Common Tunch Values** 

- **24K** → 99.9% (pure gold) 

- **22K** → 91.6% _(trade generally uses ~92% for safety)_ 

- **18K** → 75% 

## **In simple words** 

- 👉 **Tunch tells how much real gold is inside the jewellery.** 

## **Example** 

If a necklace weighs **10 grams** and has **91.6 tunch** : 

10×91.6%=9.16 grams of pure gold10 \times 91.6\% = 9.16 \text{ grams of pure gold} 10×91.6%=9.16 grams of pure gold 

## **2. What is Wastage** 

**Wastage** is the **extra percentage of gold charged** to cover: 

- Manufacturing loss 

- Craftsmanship 

- Labour and process costs 

## **Why wastage exists** 

Gold loss happens at **every stage** : 

- Melting & alloy mixing 

- Soldering 

- Stone setting 

- Filing & polishing 

## Along with: 

- Karigar labour wages 

- Electricity & fuel 

- Consumables (alloy, solder, chemicals) 

Instead of charging all these separately in , the trade ₹ **converts everything into gold wastage** . 

## **3. How wastage is charged (important clarification)** 

Wastage is charged as: 

- **Percentage** (8%, 10%, 15%, etc.) 

- Sometimes shown as **grams** 

But **in actual billing** , wastage is **NOT applied on purity** . 

## 👉 **Wastage is added directly to the tunch** . 

This is the most important point missing in basic explanations — and the key to understanding pricing. 

## **4. How Tunch + Wastage work together (Trade Method)** 

## **Core Trade Logic** 

- Jewellery has a base tunch (purity) 

- Supplier / karigar adds wastage 

- **Effective billed tunch increases** 

- Final billing is done in **fine gold** 

## **Trade Formula** 

Fine Gold Charged=Weight×(Tunch+Wastage)100\textbf{Fine Gold Charged} = \textbf{Weight} \times \frac{(\textbf{Tunch} + \textbf{Wastage})}{100}Fine Gold Charged=Weight×100(Tunch+Wastage) 

## **5. Example – 18K Jewellery (75 Tunch)** 

## **Given:** 

- Weight = **10 grams** 

- Tunch = **75** 

- Wastage = **10** 

## **Step 1: Add wastage to tunch** 

- 75 + 10 = **85** 

## **Step 2: Calculate fine gold** 

10×85100=8.5 grams10 \times \frac{85}{100} = \textbf{8.5 grams}10×10085=8.5 grams 

👉 Supplier will charge **8.5 grams of pure gold**

Although hallmark standard is **91.6%** , manufacturers usually make jewellery at **~92%** because: 

- Multiple solders & alloys are used 

- Small purity variation can happen 

- BIS hallmark rejection is a **very high cost**

## **11. Why Making Charges Increase When Gold Price Increases** 

_(Customer Question Explained Clearly)_ 

## **Customer question:** 

“Last time I bought the same design, the making was 1,000 per gram.₹ Why are you charging 1,400 or 2,000 per gram now?₹ ₹ Has the karigar increased the making?” 

## **The correct explanation** 

**The making charge has not increased because of the karigar.** It has increased **only because the gold price has increased** . 

In jewellery, **making is charged as a percentage (wastage)** , not as a fixed rupee amount.
