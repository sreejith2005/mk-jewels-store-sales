## **📜 SOP: Purchase of Old Gold – MK Jewels** 

## **📜 Objective** 

To ensure a transparent, professional, and trust-based process for accepting old gold from customers in exchange for new jewelry or credit, while maintaining compliance, customer clarity, and internal controls. 

## **📜 STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS** 

## **✅ Step 1: Initial Customer Communication & Education** 

When a customer expresses the desire to sell or exchange old gold: 

- Greet them warmly and listen carefully. 

- Clearly explain: 

“At MK Jewels, we offer the facility to purchase old gold at the same **standard gold buying rate** that we purchase from the bullion market.” 

- Emphasize: 

“This gold becomes our raw material — we buy it, refine it, and reuse it. That’s why we follow a professional and scientific process to ensure accurate purity.” 

## 🔍 **Step 2: Physical Examination** 

- Inspect each item for: 

   - Meenakari, stones, pearls, enamel, adhesives, or other non-gold elements. 

- Inform the customer clearly: 

“All non-gold elements must be removed before weighing — their value is not included in the gold rate.” 

- Ask the customer if: 

   - They would like to remove these parts themselves, 

   - Or allow you to remove them in their presence, 

   - Or give consent for MK Jewels to remove them independently. 

## **⚖️� Step 3: First Weight (Gross Weight)** 

- After removal of external elements, take the **initial gross weight** on a calibrated digital scale. 

- Let the customer know: 

“The old gold may still contain adhesive, dust, or minor non-gold content. These will get automatically eliminated during melting due to high temperature.” 

- Final calculation will be based on **post-melting weight** . 

- Record the gross weight with customer acknowledgment and explain: 

“This is the weight before melting and final purity testing.” 

## **✍️� Step 4: Consent for Melting (Customer Signature Required)** 

- Explain the importance of melting: 

“To get an accurate purity reading, the gold must be melted. In earlier times, solders used to join parts of jewelry were made of copper, silver, or other alloys. These distribute unevenly and can only mix properly after melting.” 

“Melting simply _evenly distributes_ these alloys — it is **not purification** .” 

- If the customer asks for an estimate before melting: 

“This is a rough approximation based on visual judgment. It is only for reference and **not final** . The final value will be determined **only after melting and testing** on the karatometer.” 

- Get **written and signed consent** on MK Jewels' melting form confirming: 

   - The customer understands the process is **irreversible** . 

   - The final purity may vary — it could be higher or lower than assumed. 

   - MK Jewels will not be held responsible for purity differences post melting. 

## **📜 Step 5: Melting Process** 

- Melt the gold using standard procedure in: 

   - The presence of the customer, or 

   - With their verbal or written consent for melting without being present. 

- Record both **pre- and post-melting weights** . 

- Inform: 

“Now the gold is in a uniform state where purity can be tested accurately.” 

## 📊 **Step 6: Testing on Karatometer** 

- Use **karatometer** to assess purity of the melted sample. 

- Clarify: 

“Karatometer results may vary slightly (1–2%) depending on the technology and machine brand.” 

“This is a standard procedure — the purity reflects what the gold **originally was** when purchased from any jeweler.” 

“We are just facilitators, helping you get a scientific and fair value. If the result is less or more, it reflects the original karigar’s or jeweller’s craftsmanship at the time of purchase.” 

## **📜 Step 7: Purity Declaration & Rate Explanation** 

- Example: If the purity reads **79%** , explain: 

“We will consider **78%** for final valuation, as 1% is deducted as a standard **refining charge** . This is not our profit or margin — it's an industry-wide processing deduction due to melting loss.” 

- Reinforce: 

“We do not earn a margin in purchasing old gold. This is a **customer service facility** , not a revenue stream for us.” 

## **📜 Step 8: Final Valuation & Estimate** 

- Final value = Post-melting weight × Assessed purity (%) × Today’s standard gold buying rate. 

- If a customer had previously asked for an estimate: 

“The earlier quote was an approximate value. This final figure is based on scientific testing and is the accurate value.” 

## 💰 **Step 9: Issuing Credit or Exchange** 

- Offer two options: 

   1. **Credit note** for purchasing new jewelry (preferred). 

   2. **Exchange** against new jewelry purchase at MK Jewels (excluding coins or bullion). 

   3. **Bank transfer** (only applicable if the jewelry is being purchased from MK Jewels). 

- Clarify: 

“The gold rate applied is our **buying rate** , which is usually **better** than most exchange offers from outside jewelers.” 

## **📜 Step 10: Documentation & Record-Keeping** 

Maintain a customer file with: 

- Detailed item description 

- Pre- and post-melting weights 

- Karatometer report and purity % 

- Applied gold rate 

- Final value credited 

- Valid customer ID proof 

- Signatures of both **customer** and **MK Jewels representative** 

## **📜 Bonus Talking Points for Sales Executives** 

Use these to build confidence during conversation: 

- “You are receiving full and fair value as per today’s live gold rate.” 

- “There are no hidden deductions — everything is explained and documented.” 

- “Melting is not for our benefit, but to ensure **your protection** and accurate valuation.” 

- “We want you to feel confident and respected throughout this process — that’s our priority.” 

## **⚠️� Important Reminders** 

- **Never** commit any estimate as final without melting. 

- Avoid accepting gold with **non-removable stones** , unless clearly agreed upon by the customer. 

- Keep the customer informed and comfortable at every stage. 

- Always take **two signatures** : one for melting consent, one for final transaction.
